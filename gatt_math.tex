\subsection*{Group Average Treatment Effect on the Treated (GATT)}

Let $D_i \in \{0,1\}$ denote treatment status, $T_i \in \{0,1\}$ a time indicator,
and $G_i \in \mathcal G$ a discrete group variable.
We are interested in the group-specific average treatment effect on the treated
\[
\tau_g \;=\; \mathbb E\!\left[ Y_i(1) - Y_i(0) \mid D_i=1,\, G_i=g \right].
\]
We start from a doubly robust score for the overall ATT,
which yields 
\[
\theta^{ATT}
=
-\frac{\mathbb E[\psi_b]}{\mathbb E[\psi_a]}.
\]

To obtain a group-specific ATT, we do not condition on $D_i=1$.
Instead, we define a \emph{group-weighted moment condition} using the indicator
$I_{g,i} = \mathbf 1\{G_i=g\}$:
\[
\mathbb E\!\left[ I_{g,i}\,(\psi_{a,i}\,\tau_g + \psi_{b,i}) \right] = 0.
\]
Solving for $\tau_g$ gives
\[
\boxed{
\tau_g
=
-\frac{\mathbb E[I_g\,\psi_b]}{\mathbb E[I_g\,\psi_a]}
}
\]
which is the GATT estimator implied by the same score that identifies the ATT.
